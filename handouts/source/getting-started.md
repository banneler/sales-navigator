# Getting Started — Handout

*Sales-Navigator · Great Plains Communications · Internal training*

## What this hub is

**Sales-Navigator** is the interactive training surface for **GPC sales onboarding**: modules, practice **scenarios**, and **knowledge checks** in one place. The left **sidebar** lists every module, grouped **by category**; the active module is highlighted. Use the **chevron** on the sidebar to collapse it when you need more reading room.

## Inside a typical module

- **Title and summary** at the top set context.
- **Coffee Summary** bullets give a fast scan when the module includes them.
- Major topics appear as **section cards**—each `##` heading in the source becomes its own card.
- Some modules add **reference links** (SharePoint, PDFs) in a side column on wide screens.
- **Scenarios** sit beside the main column when present: a short situation, a few choices, and feedback—practice for real customer *or internal* moments (e.g. finding collateral), not a graded test.
- **Knowledge checks** often appear in a carousel toward the bottom: quick questions with explanations so you can self-check without pressure.

## Tour behavior

On **Scenarios**, **Knowledge checks**, and **Fiber path** tour steps, **Next** stays disabled until you complete the highlighted activity (pick the sample answers, or open Fiber path)—this mirrors how real modules gate progress until you engage the practice.

## Scenario drill (collateral)

**Situation:** You need the most recent **Unified Communications** product collateral and are not sure where it is.

| Response | Why it matters |
|----------|----------------|
| Email **Everyone@gpcom.com** | Wrong—broadcast noise; official sources exist. |
| **Sales SharePoint → Sales Resources** | **Correct**—central hub for current collateral. |
| Badger a colleague relentlessly | Wrong—respect people’s time; use the hub first. |

**Coach takeaway:** Official hubs beat broadcast email; Sales Resources keeps everyone from playing hide-and-seek with documents.

## Knowledge check (Blair — humor)

Sample question references **Blair, NE** dining (e.g. **El Vallarta** vs alternatives). These items are explicitly **not graded**—they reinforce that checks can be light touch.

## Executive Map Book (when enabled)

If **Executive Map Book** appears in the nav, it opens **full-width** in the main area: digital map-book style views for executive conversations on footprint, reach, and strategy. Same app chrome (header + sidebar) as modules; switch back to any module from the sidebar.
