## Acronyms & products

- **SIA** — Standard Internet Access (shared, cost-sensitive, best-effort latency).
- **DIA** — Dedicated Internet Access (symmetrical throughput, dedicated path, richer **DDoS** visibility, **NOC**-style support).
- **Managed Ethernet**, **E-Line**, **EPL**, **EVPL**, **E-LAN**, **EP-LAN**, **EVP-LAN**.
- **Wave / Wavelength** — **Layer 1** optical; **DC–DC**, hub-to-hub; **not** a substitute for DIA/SIA Internet.

## DIA vs SIA (comparison themes)

| Dimension | SIA | DIA |
|-----------|-----|-----|
| Throughput (sheet) | Up to **~2 Gbps** symmetrical | Up to **100 Gbps** symmetrical |
| Static IPs | **1** | **3** |
| Security | Network-level DDoS | **Customer-specific** DDoS monitoring/mitigation |
| Latency | Standard / best-effort | **Premium** low-latency with stated objectives |
| Support | **Tier 1 ISC** | **NOC-centric** enhanced support |
| Access diversity | Standard | **Multiple diverse** options where applicable |
| **Nature** | **Shared** | **Dedicated** fiber |

## Managed Ethernet (positioning)

- Private **Layer 2** site-to-site (not Internet breakout).
- **MEF-certified** fiber; **16,500-mile** GPC footprint positioning.
- Speeds up to **100 Gbps**; scalable **10 Mbps–10 Gbps**; **29+** data centers / carrier hotels across Midwest metros (Omaha, Denver, Kansas City, Minneapolis, Des Moines, Chicago, Indianapolis, etc.).
- **SLA** with **24×7×365 NOC** (**Blair, NE**).
- vs nationwide fiber competitors: **distinct Midwest routes**, **local** engagement; private Ethernet **isolated** from public Internet vs Internet VPN; **cost-per-bit** vs legacy **MPLS** positioning.

## Wave / wavelength

- **10 Gbps–400 Gbps**; **24×7** support from **Blair NOC**; handoff **10, 100, or 400 Gbps** on **2-fiber single-mode** where required.
- Discovery: endpoints, distance/regions, transport, growth to **100G/400G**, diversity, Layer 1 vs routed IP.

## Fiber vs LEO / satellite (Starlink-class)

- **Illustrative latency** (training): fiber **~5–15 ms** vs Starlink **~21–30 ms** (not a contractual SLA—use for discovery).
- **Satellite pain themes:** weather/obstruction sensitivity; variable latency under load; shared-airtime congestion; rooftop/dish constraints; self-install risk.
- **Fiber response:** enterprise-grade reliability; consistent ultra-low latency for **POS/VoIP/cloud/VPN**; scalable dedicated bandwidth; professional install + local + **NOC** support.
- **Ideal-fit businesses:** high upload demand; predictable POS/VoIP/cloud; public-sector/education; long-term scalable landline replacement; rural sites where environmental factors hurt satellite.

## Discovery checklist (access)

- Apps, upload vs download, static IPs, diversity, **SLA** expectations, legacy (**cable, fiber, T1/MPLS**, wireless), growth **24–36 months**.
- DIA vs SIA: MPLS, VPN over broadband, direct Internet; congestion; DDoS/outages; performance reporting for IT/auditors.

## Competitive behavior

- Do not trash competitors; anchor **validated design** and **customer outcomes**.
- Competitive references in training: **Starlink**, **MPLS**, **VPN**, **ISC**, **NOC**, **UNI**—use only to structure discovery, not as insults.
