## Positioning principle

Lead with **business outcomes** (uptime, growth, cloud performance, risk), not SKU lists. GPC is positioned as **fiber-first access**, a **resilient Midwest IP network**, and a **partner** (not a commodity ISP): **local teams**, **24/7 network monitoring**, and **more than a century** of operating experience.

**Proof themes:** purpose-built IP reach; **Tier-1** providers and **Internet exchanges (IX)**; paths tuned for **cloud** and **SaaS**; end-user experience—not just “speeds and feeds.”

## Deal frame

Use **“access + path + policy + apps”** against **sites and workloads**:

1. **Locations** and criticality.
2. **Applications** and sensitivity (latency, uptime, compliance).
3. **How sites connect today** and what breaks at scale.
4. Map needs to the **layered stack** below—summarize in **one sentence** before deep product talk.

## Layered portfolio map

| Layer | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| Access | **Fiber Internet**; **Wireless Internet Backup** |
| WAN | **Managed Ethernet**; **SD-WAN** |
| Security | **Managed Firewall**; **DDoS Protection** |
| Apps & edge | **Unified Communications**; **Cloud Connect**; **Managed Wi-Fi**; **Video** |

Discovery prompts: **locations**; **critical apps**; **uptime tolerance**; **cloud/voice moves**; **firewall policy**; **Wi-Fi guest vs corporate**; **direct cloud paths vs public internet only**.

## Differentiators (talk track)

- **Midwest** foundation and **accountable local support** vs national commodity narratives.
- **24/7 monitoring** + local teams vs “remote-only” support models.
- Full **layered** services so conversations stay on **outcomes** and **proof** (references, NOC, redundancy), not single-product price.

## Collateral

- **Business Capability Presentation** (source deck) and the **deep collateral library** (datasheets by product/service—Internet, Ethernet, SD-WAN, UC, etc.) are cited in the module; pull the **current** versions from **Sales Resources** / marketing-approved folders—not ad-hoc files.

## Landmines

- Do not invent positioning that contradicts **marketing** or **product management**—stay aligned with approved battle cards and datasheets.
- When the deal crosses domains (security, WAN, voice), **bring specialists** (SE, PM, quoting)—don’t wing full architecture on a first call.
