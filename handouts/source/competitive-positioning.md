# Competitive Positioning — Handout

*Sales-Navigator · Great Plains Communications*

**References:** **Product battle cards** and **Competitive battle cards** on **Sales SharePoint → Sales Resources** (anchors in the module). Confirm **currency** of any stat before **external** use.

## Core framework

1. **Discovery before claims** — Applications, sites, outcomes (POS/VoIP/video, upload vs download, uptime/SLA, multi-location, how sites connect today, backhaul vs local breakout, migration timelines). Map strengths/weaknesses **after** you understand their reality—avoid generic “we’re better.”
2. **Competitor → GPC response** — For each angle: what they say / what’s true in their pitch → **business risk or gap for this customer** → **your proof** (local teams, **24×7 NOC**, redundant fiber, symmetrical speeds, contract stability, portfolio, references)—use only what fits discovery.
3. **Proof-point patterns** — Network attributes (buried vs aerial, redundancy, leased vs owned where relevant), service model (local install/support vs remote-only), commercial terms (locks, fees, renewals), experience metrics (references, tenure, NPS where appropriate). Lead with **outcomes**, not slides.

## Landmines (hard stops)

- No **personal** attacks on competitors.
- Do **not** cite battle-card **stats externally** without confirming **currency** with marketing.
- Avoid **absolutes** (“never,” “always”) on competitor networks.
- **Acknowledge** where a competitor is legitimately strong; reframe to **your fit**.
- **Architectural deals** (SD-WAN, security, multi-site, complex cloud pathing): stay in discovery; bring **SE/PM**—don’t improvise topology.

## When to pull SE or PM

- **SE early:** multi-site WAN/SD-WAN or MPLS migration; integrated security/firewall; off-net + on-net hybrid; DDoS or regulated workloads; fiber feasibility or diverse paths; RFP-style technical validation.
- **PM:** delivery timeline, construction, contract alignment.

## Metro & local competition

- Counter is **not price alone**—fit, experience, operational reliability.
- **Local overbuilders / nationals:** may lead on price, speed tiers, or speed of build—probe **install quality**, **support model** (local vs remote), **redundancy**, **owned vs leased** facilities.
- **Omaha metro:** Entrants may emphasize quick builds and community marketing; test support consistency, local depth, aerial vs buried quality, post-merger stability.
- **T-Mobile / Metronet:** Separate consumer brand story from B2B; test support consistency, local depth, aerial vs buried, post-merger stability.
- vs “lowest advertised price,” tie to **total cost of downtime**, billing surprises, lack of local escalation.

## Fiber vs satellite (LEO / Starlink)

**Satellite pain themes:** weather/obstruction sensitivity; variable latency under load; shared-airtime congestion; rooftop/dish constraints; self-install risk.

**Fiber response:** enterprise-grade reliability; consistent ultra-low latency for POS/VoIP/cloud/VPN; scalable dedicated bandwidth; professional install + local + NOC support.

**Qualifying:** employees/devices at site; critical apps; SLA needs; upload-heavy workloads; public-sector/education; rural sites needing environmental stability.

**“Starlink is everywhere”:** acknowledge coverage; reframe to **reliability, latency, operational support** for **business** applications.

## M&A & Cox / Charter / Spectrum narrative

- Prospects want **stability, service quality, honest comparison**—not gossip.
- **GPC themes:** long-tenure regional operator; redundant fiber monitored **24×7**; local sales and support in-market. Third-party scores: **directional** only—**confirm before external quoting**.
- **Comparison dimensions:** access technology (fiber depth vs coax-centric), symmetry and headline speeds, ease of doing business, renewal/price-lock behavior, fees, local vs offshore support narratives.
- **“Combined company benefits”:** acknowledge competitor commitments; pivot to what GPC already delivers (support model continuity, monitoring, consistency). **Factual, customer-centric** tone.

## Technology battle cards (SD-WAN pattern)

**Flow:** Pain → GPC wrap → qualifying questions → national-carrier challenge and response.

**SD-WAN qualifiers:** site count; MPLS/VPN/backhaul behavior; bandwidth per site; cloud vs DC apps; **12–24 month** horizon; mission-critical SaaS.

**Proof areas:** centralized orchestration/visibility; application-aware routing; security integration; performance over varied underlays; nationwide reach with local engagement—**verify awards/ratings (year and scope) before citing.**

Apply the same **pain → wrap → questions → proof** pattern to other technology cards; bring **SE** for design validation instead of debating from the card alone.

## Rebuttal discipline

Tie rebuttals to **outcomes** (uptime, latency, support model, **total cost of downtime**)—not price or speed tests alone. Keep acquisition talk **factual** and **customer-centric**.
