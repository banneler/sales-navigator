# Sales Rules of Engagement — Handout

*Sales-Navigator · Great Plains Communications*

**Authoritative source:** *Sales Rules of Engagement (ROE)* — current revision on **Sales SharePoint** (see module **Reference Files**). **Strategic Accounts** list is maintained separately; use the **official** ROE repository, not informal spreadsheets.

## System of record & data discipline

- **Salesforce** is the system of record. **Search before you create** accounts or contacts—duplicate entities and contacts drive ownership fights and bad reporting.
- **Leadership** uses Salesforce to resolve conflicts—**data quality** and **timely logging** matter as much as outreach.
- When creating: search by **name and address**; do **not** create a second account for the same entity; if a contact’s email already exists on another record, coordinate with the owning rep—**no duplicate contacts** across records.

## Activity windows (general / non-strategic)

| Record type | Inactivity threshold | Effect |
|-------------|------------------------|--------|
| **Prospect** | **61+** days without qualifying activity | Others may work the record |
| **Customer** | **121+** days without qualifying activity | Others may work the record |

**Activity** is **not** “notes only.” You need **consistent contact** (e.g. at least **every ~60 days** for prospects in rhythm) and **current intel** (projects, contract timing, stakeholders).

## Inbound leads (sales or marketing assigned)

- First **contact** and a **logged touch** in Salesforce within **5 business days**, or the lead may be **reassigned**.
- That clock is **not** the same rule as **self-sourced** prospecting—do not confuse the two.

## Strategic Accounts & E-Rate / USAC

- **Strategic Accounts** (official list): protected by **assignment**, **not** by the 61/121-day general rules.
- **E-Rate / USAC-funded entities** are treated as **Strategic** for ROE purposes **even if** not on the named list—**exception:** **ENA (Education Networks of America) in Indiana** stays with the **Out-State Team** per ROE.
- **Who owns the list:** **Sales Managers** and **VP of Sales**; changes are communicated from the **official Sales SharePoint** ROE materials—**informal lists are not authoritative**.

## Transfers & edge cases

- **Never** reassign unilaterally in Salesforce. Talk to the **current owner** first; then get **email approval** from **your manager** and **sales leadership**.
- Roughly **~20%** of situations may not fit ROE cleanly—**escalate to sales management case by case**; do not improvise policy.
- Cross-team friction on ownership or E-Rate/USAC coverage: use **leadership** and **Salesforce history** as evidence.

## “What good looks like”

Search before create; **one account per entity**; meaningful activity rhythm; even when a record looks “open” by the numbers, **reach the listed owner first** before engaging the account.

## Scenario cues (training)

- **130+ days** idle, not Strategic, not E-Rate/USAC: still **coordinate with the owner / proper transfer path**—not “start without contacting the incumbent.”
- Marketing lead assigned **Monday**; **following Tuesday** = **six business days** with **no** Salesforce log—may be reassigned; **email alone** ≠ logged activity where ROE requires a logged touch.

## Knowledge-check anchors

- **5 business days** for assigned inbound first contact + log (vs self-sourced).
- Strategic list authority: **Sales Managers + VP of Sales**; authoritative list: **Sales SharePoint** per ROE.
- E-Rate/USAC: Strategic-style treatment; **not** ordinary 61/121.
- Transfers: incumbent + manager + leadership **in writing**.
