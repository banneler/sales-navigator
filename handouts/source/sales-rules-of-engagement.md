## At a glance

| Topic | Rule (general path) |
| --- | --- |
| **Prospect** idle | **61+** days with no qualifying logged activity → others may work the account |
| **Customer** idle | **121+** days → others may work |
| **Assigned inbound** | First contact **and** Salesforce log within **5 business days** |
| **Self-sourced leads** | **Not** subject to the same 5-day clock as sales/marketing-assigned inbound |

---

## The reality check

The Rules of Engagement exist for **one reason**: to protect everyone’s pipeline and keep **clear boundaries** across the team. **Salesforce is the undisputed system of record** — not Outlook, not chat, not side agreements.

### The core mandates

| Mandate | In practice |
| --- | --- |
| **Search before you create** | One entity, one account. Duplicates drive ownership fights, bad reporting, and **commission risk**. |
| **Log it or lose it** | Tasks, calls, meetings, or emails must live **on the Salesforce record**. Your **Sent** folder does not count in a dispute. |
| **Transfer through leadership** | Never reassign an account **unilaterally**. Align with the current owner, then **written** approval through your manager and sales leadership. |

Strategic accounts and **E-Rate / USAC-funded** entities follow **different** rules than the general 61/121 path. Know which path applies **before** you engage.

---

## System of record & data discipline

- **Search** by name and address before creating accounts or contacts.
- **Leadership** resolves conflicts from Salesforce — prioritize **data quality**, **timely logging**, and **meaningful activity** on the record.
- **No parallel accounts** to bypass an incumbent owner. If you need access, use the **transfer path**.

---

## Idle windows *(general / non-strategic only)*

**Qualifying activity** = logged on the account in Salesforce (not email alone).

| Record type | Threshold | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Prospect** | **61+** days no qualifying activity | Others may work the account |
| **Customer** | **121+** days | Others may work the account |

Even when a window is **open by the numbers**, **reach the listed owner first** — then use formal transfer steps if needed. **No cowboy grabs.**

---

## Assigned inbound — the 5-day clock

> **Five business days** from assignment: make **first contact** and log it in **Salesforce**. Miss it, and the lead may be **re-routed**.  
> This clock applies to **sales- or marketing-assigned inbound** — **not** the same rule as **self-sourced** prospecting.

---

## Strategic accounts & E-Rate / USAC

| Area | Rule |
| --- | --- |
| **Strategic Accounts** (official list) | **Assignment** and list mechanics — **not** the generic 61/121 idle path |
| **Who owns the official list** | **Sales Managers** and **VP of Sales**; published via **official Sales SharePoint** (per ROE) |
| **E-Rate / USAC-funded entities** | **Strategic-style** treatment **even if** not on the named list (see ROE for documented exceptions, e.g. **ENA Indiana → Out-State Team**) |
| **When unsure** | Confirm with **leadership** — do **not** assume casual 61/121 applies |

## Transfers & edge cases

**Approved path (summary)**

1. Talk to the **current owner** — get alignment.  
2. Involve **your manager** and **sales leadership**.  
3. Secure **written** approval **before** ownership changes.  
4. **Never** reassign in Salesforce first to “force” a deal.

Roughly **~20%** of edge cases will not fit the ROE cleanly — **escalate to sales management** case by case; do not improvise policy. Use **Salesforce history** as evidence, not Slack polls or rumors.

## Landmines *(deal-killers)*

| If you hear yourself thinking… | The reality |
| --- | --- |
| *“I emailed them twice—that counts.”* | **Sent Folder Delusion** — if it is not logged as a task, call, meeting, or email **on the record**, it **did not happen** for ROE purposes. |
| *“130 days idle — I’ll just start working it.”* | **Cowboy Grab** — window may be open, but **contact the listed owner first**; use leadership transfer if needed. |
| *“I’ll reassign it to myself in Salesforce.”* | **Rogue Reassignment** — **major violation**. Align + **written** leadership path only. |
| *“School district = 61/121.”* | **E-Rate Trap** — USAC/E-Rate entities get **Strategic-style** treatment unless leadership confirms otherwise. |
| *“Inbound lead — I’ll get to it next week.”* | **5-Day Clock** — **five business days**, period, for assigned inbound **contact + log**. |
| *“New account record is faster than talking to the owner.”* | **Duplicate Disaster** — search first; parallel records **break reporting** and **commissions**. |

## Scenarios *(training cues)*

### 1 — The 130-Day Ghost Town

**Situation** — **Customer** account; **not** Strategic; **not** E-Rate. **Zero** Salesforce activity **130** days. You want to reach out **today**.

| Choice | Coach’s line |
| --- | --- |
| **Rookie move** | “Past 121 — I’m dialing **now**.” → *Internal friction.* Window may allow outreach, but **reach the listed owner first**. |
| **Strong move** | **Coordinate** with the owner; if unresponsive, **ROE transfer steps through leadership** before you “claim” the account. |

### 2 — The “Sent Folder” Defense

**Situation** — Inbound assigned **Monday**. **Day 6** (next week): **no** Salesforce activity. You: *“But I emailed them twice from Outlook!”*

| Choice | Coach’s line |
| --- | --- |
| **Wrong** | “Outlook proves I’m working it.” → **Dead wrong.** **Sent** doesn’t pay out. **Five business days** for **contact + Salesforce log**. |
| **Right** | If it is **not logged in Salesforce**, leadership can treat it as **not done** — log **going forward** and clarify with your manager if the lead moved. |

---

## Knowledge-check anchors

- **5 business days** — first contact **+** log for **assigned inbound** only (vs self-sourced).  
- **121+** — general **Customer** idle; **61+** — general **Prospect** idle.  
- **Strategic list** — **Sales Managers + VP of Sales**; **Sales SharePoint** per ROE.  
- **E-Rate / USAC** — **Strategic-style**; **not** casual 61/121.  
- **Transfers** — incumbent → manager → leadership **in writing**.
