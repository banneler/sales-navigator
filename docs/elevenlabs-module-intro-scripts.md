# Module intro scripts (ElevenLabs)

Use with the **Play module intro** control in the opening preview modal. Target ~**140 words per minute**; lengths below are approximate.

**Editor’s note (consistency pass):**

- Openings alternate slightly (“In this module,” “Here’s the focus,” “This one is about”) so playback back-to-back does not sound copy-pasted.
- Every script uses **second person** and ends with a **soft invitation** to continue—no UI language (“click,” “button”).
- **You’ll learn** / **you’ll see** / **you’ll leave with** are rotated for rhythm; closes use a small set of phrases, not identical sign-offs.
- Acronyms: expanded **once** where needed for voice; dense stacks (BGP, IDS/IPS) shortened or folded into plain language where possible.
- **GPC** and **Salesforce** are used as proper names; product names match the module content.

## competitive-positioning

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | Competitive Positioning |
| **words** | ~132 |
| **~seconds** | ~56 |

**Script**

In competitive deals, credibility comes from tying proof to what the customer already told you—not from the loudest claim. Here’s the focus: a disciplined framework for positioning GPC when another vendor is in the room. You’ll learn to lead with discovery, then map each competitor angle to business risk for *this* account and proof you can back up with local teams, the network operations center, redundancy, and portfolio breadth. You’ll see the landmines clearly: no personal attacks, no absolutes, and no battle-card statistics in front of a customer until marketing has confirmed the numbers. You’ll also get practical reframes for metro and local competition, fiber versus low Earth orbit satellite, and merger-and-acquisition noise—anchored on uptime, latency, support, and total cost of downtime, not a speed test alone. When you’re ready, step in and turn pressure into a story that fits their reality.

## product-connectivity

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | Connectivity & Access |
| **words** | ~130 |
| **~seconds** | ~56 |

**Script**

Everything upstream—voice, security, and cloud—depends on how you qualify and position the pipe. In this module you’ll keep Standard Internet Access, or SIA, Dedicated Internet Access, or DIA, private Ethernet, and wavelength in the right mental boxes. You’ll learn when shared SIA fits budget-conscious needs, and when DIA earns the conversation for symmetrical throughput, stronger operations, and better visibility into denial-of-service risk. You’ll place managed Ethernet as private Layer-2 connectivity between sites—not the same sale as public internet—and reserve wavelength for high-capacity hub-and-data-center pipes when discovery supports it, not as a default for every branch. You’ll compare fiber to satellite on reliability and latency without sounding dismissive, and you’ll keep internal numbers internal until quoting blesses customer-facing figures. Move forward when you want a tighter bandwidth story and cleaner handoffs to pricing.

## product-cloud-wifi-and-backup

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | Cloud, Wi-Fi & Backup |
| **words** | ~126 |
| **~seconds** | ~54 |

**Script**

When cloud apps and branch uptime decide whether you win, the story has to match what you can actually stand behind. This module covers three pillars: cloud path, managed Wi‑Fi, and wireless backup. You’ll learn how Cloud Connect with managed Ethernet offers a private path to major clouds and SaaS, and when to align cross-connects, diversity, and security with a solutions engineer before you over-promise. You’ll position managed Wi‑Fi with guest and corporate separation and a real security stack—without quoting access point counts until a site survey. You’ll set fair expectations for fifth-generation wireless backup: automatic failover, NOC support, optional battery, and why speeds and tiers vary by site. Whenever you’re ready, continue and build a conversation you can defend in front of engineering and the customer.

## product-security-and-sd-wan

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | Security & SD-WAN |
| **words** | ~133 |
| **~seconds** | ~57 |

**Script**

In security and SD-WAN, one fuzzy line item can unwind trust on the whole quote. Here’s the focus: match product to risk, and know when to pull specialists in early. You’ll learn how managed firewall fits alongside Managed Network Security—the broader umbrella that can include managing customer-owned gear, SIEM, assessments, and email security depending on scope. You’ll see why proactive distributed denial-of-service protection is not the same as on-demand mitigation windows on the on-demand SKU, and why that distinction has to be explicit before signature. You’ll practice SD-WAN discovery for multi-site designs with at least one GPC fiber site, and you’ll stop assuming advanced firewall or intrusion-prevention features are in the base quote until solutions engineering confirms. You’ll leave with clear handoff triggers for high availability, complex routing, and regulated environments. Step through the material when you’re ready to move from buzzwords to a scope you can quote.

## product-uc-voice-and-collaboration

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | UC, Voice & Collaboration |
| **words** | ~128 |
| **~seconds** | ~55 |

**Script**

Voice, messaging, meetings, and contact center often sit in the same conversation as Microsoft Teams—and licensing surprises are painful for everyone. This module keeps the story accurate. You’ll learn how the unified communications platform bundles voice, messaging, SMS and MMS, meetings, optional contact center, and files—and how tiers like Unite Enterprise differ on storage so expectations match the brochure. You’ll clarify that Microsoft Teams and Phone System licenses sit alongside GPC’s cloud PBX story, not inside one mystery SKU, so customers see the joint picture early. For contact center, you’ll respect concurrent licensing for Contact Center Pro and Elite and keep bursting and elastic seats with account executive and pricing—not a napkin estimate. For customers not ready for full UC, you’ll touch SIP, PRI, and plain old telephone service paths with the right discovery. Continue when you’re ready and keep renewals out of awkward territory.

## sales-rules-of-engagement

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | Sales Rules of Engagement |
| **words** | ~135 |
| **~seconds** | ~58 |

**Script**

When more than one seller touches the same account, unclear rules waste time and erode trust. The Sales Rules of Engagement keep ownership fair and Salesforce the system of record when disputes come up. In this module you’ll build habits: search before you create accounts and contacts, and understand idle windows—more than sixty-one days without qualifying activity for prospects, and more than a hundred twenty-one days for customers on the general path—plus the five-business-day clock for sales- or marketing-assigned inbound leads, separate from what you self-source. You’ll see how Strategic accounts work, how E-Rate and USAC-funded entities follow Strategic-style treatment, and why moving an account takes alignment with the current owner and written approval through your manager and sales leadership—not side deals in chat. Work through the lessons at your pace until the rules feel automatic.

## sales-operations-and-approvals

| | |
| --- | --- |
| **title** | Sales Operations & Approvals |
| **words** | ~124 |
| **~seconds** | ~53 |

**Script**

Nothing stalls a deal like the wrong approval step—or a change package sent after billing has already started. This module helps you navigate GPC Solutions with confidence. You’ll use Approval History on the record to approve, reject, reassign, or recall with comments your reviewers can trust. You’ll separate a change order after Closed Won but before billing from Move-Add-Change work after billing is live, and from disconnect when services come out entirely. You’ll know when Request Manual Validation on a Solution Site pulls a case out of automation for deeper review, and how Operational Business Review prep fits alongside—but never replaces—Salesforce approvals and costing discipline. Settle in and work the material so ops conversations stay crisp and your customers stay protected.

**Script**

A clean Salesforce rhythm turns leads into opportunities, signed agreements, and implementations that start without scrambling.
